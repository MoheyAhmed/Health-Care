Learn quick and essential first aid tips to help you handle medical emergencies safely and confidently. From breathing difficulties to minor injuries, our guides empower you to act fast and protect health when it matters most.

Shortness of Breath

Steps for Shortness of Breath

  • Stay calm and reassure the person.
  • Sit them upright (not lying down).
  • Loosen tight clothing.
  • Help them take slow breaths.
  • Use prescribed inhalers if they have them.
  • Call emergency services if symptoms are severe, worsening, or accompanied by chest pain, blue lips, or confusion.

Diabetic Coma

Steps for Diabetic coma

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Check for medical ID (bracelet, necklace, card) indicating diabetes.
  • Do not give food or drink if the person is unconscious.
  • If conscious and alert, offer a sugary drink or glucose tablets if they’re having low blood sugar.
  • Keep them safe and comfortable until help arrives.
  • Monitor breathing and responsiveness. Be ready to give CPR if necessary.

Headache

Steps for headache

  • Move to a quiet, comfortable place.
  • Encourage rest and relaxation.
  • Offer water to stay hydrated.
  • Use a cool cloth on the forehead or neck.
  • Avoid bright lights and loud noises.
  • Give pain relief medication if appropriate and safe for the person.
  • Seek medical help if the headache is sudden, severe, or accompanied by weakness, vision changes, or confusion.

Earache

Steps for Earache

  • Keep the person comfortable and calm.
  • Apply a warm cloth to the outside of the ear for pain relief.
  • Offer pain medication if appropriate and safe (e.g. paracetamol, ibuprofen).
  • Keep the ear dry — avoid getting water in it.
  • Do not insert objects into the ear.
  • Seek medical help if pain is severe, there’s fever, fluid draining, hearing loss, or if symptoms last more than 2 days.
